# Soil Cloning: The Golden Mean

![Soil Cloning](/images/guides/soil-cloning.jpg)

Soil cloning (or coco) is the best balance between speed, cost, and success. Faster than water (7-14 days), cheaper than aerocloner ($5-10), 60-80% success. Perfect for intermediate level!

## The Method

**How it works:**
1. Cut clone
2. Dip in rooting hormone
3. Plant in soil/coco
4. Maintain 80-90% humidity
5. After 7-14 days ‚Äî roots!

**Simplicity:** 7/10 (bit harder than water)
**Speed:** 8/10 (2x faster than water)
**Success:** 8/10 (beginners 60-70%, experienced 80-95%)

## What You Need

### Essential

1. **Medium:**
   - Option 1: Light soil (50% perlite + 50% peat)
   - Option 2: Coco (pure or with perlite 70/30)
   - Option 3: Rapid Rooter / Jiffy (ready cubes)

2. **Cups:**
   - Volume: 7-17 oz (0.2-0.5 L)
   - With drainage holes
   - Transparent (to see roots through walls)

3. **Rooting hormone:**
   - **Gel:** Clonex Gel ($15-25, best)
   - **Powder:** Root Riot, Hormex ($5-15, cheaper)

4. **Sharp blade:**
   - Sterile! (alcohol required)

5. **Dome/humidity box:**
   - For 80-95% humidity
   - Can use: transparent container with lid
   - Or: plastic bottle (cut)

6. **Spray bottle:**
   - To maintain humidity

### Optional (but recommended)

7. **Heating mat:**
   - Maintains soil temp 72-75¬∞F (22-24¬∞C)
   - $20-40
   - Speeds rooting by 2-3 days

8. **Low light:**
   - CFL 20-40W or LED 50-100W
   - Don't need bright light!

## Choosing Medium

### 1. Light Soil

**Composition:**
- 50% perlite + 50% peat
- Or: 40% perlite + 40% peat + 20% vermiculite

**Pros:**
- Airy (roots grow fast)
- Cheap ($5-10)
- Clone immediately adapts to soil

**Cons:**
- Need to mix yourself
- Risk of overwatering (need experience)

**For:** Intermediate level

---

### 2. Coco

**Composition:**
- 100% coco
- Or: 70% coco + 30% perlite

**Pros:**
- Excellent aeration
- Hard to overwater
- Fast rooting (7-10 days)

**Cons:**
- Need to rinse before use
- Slightly more expensive ($10-15)

**For:** All levels

---

### 3. Rapid Rooter / Jiffy

**What it is:**
- Rapid Rooter: sponge from peat/organic ($10-20 for 50)
- Jiffy: peat pellet ($5-10 for 50)

**Pros:**
- Ready to use (no preparation needed)
- Perfect aeration
- High success (80-90%)

**Cons:**
- More expensive than soil/coco
- Single-use

**For:** All levels (especially beginners)

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Prepare Medium

**Soil/coco:**
1. Mix medium (if needed)
2. Moisten with water pH 5.5-6.0
3. Don't flood! Should be moist, not wet
4. Fill cups

**Rapid Rooter/Jiffy:**
1. Soak in water pH 5.5-6.0
2. Let excess water drain
3. Ready!

---

### Step 2: Prepare Tools

1. **Clean blade:**
   - Wipe with alcohol
   - Let dry

2. **Prepare hormone:**
   - Gel: squeeze into clean lid
   - Powder: pour into lid

3. **Prepare dome:**
   - Wash
   - Wipe dry

---

### Step 3: Cut Clone

**Choose branch:**
‚úÖ Lower/middle branches
‚úÖ Thickness: pencil-sized
‚úÖ Length: 4-6 inches (10-15 cm / 4-6 nodes)

**Cutting technique:**

1. **At 45¬∞ angle:**
   - Sharp blade (not scissors!)
   - Quick, one motion

2. **Between nodes:**
   - 0.2 inch (5 mm) below node

3. **Immediately into water:**
   - Prepare glass of water beforehand
   - Clone in water while preparing others

**Processing:**

4. **Remove lower leaves:**
   - Keep 2-3 top fan leaves
   - Remove all leaves on lower 2 inches (5 cm)

5. **Trim top leaves:**
   - Cut by 50% (optional)
   - Reduces moisture loss

---

### Step 4: Hormone Treatment

**Gel (Clonex):**
1. Remove clone from water
2. Dip cut in gel 0.4-0.8 inch (1-2 cm)
3. Hold 2-3 seconds
4. Immediately place in medium

**Powder:**
1. Remove clone from water
2. Shake excess water (but cut should be moist!)
3. Dip cut in powder
4. Shake off excess powder
5. Place in medium

‚ö†Ô∏è **Important:** Don't let cut dry between treatment and planting!

---

### Step 5: Planting

**In soil/coco:**
1. Make hole (pencil/stick)
2. Depth: 1-2 inches (3-5 cm)
3. Place clone
4. Gently press medium around stem
5. DON'T compact too hard!

**In Rapid Rooter:**
1. Sponge already has hole
2. Place clone
3. Lightly squeeze sponge around stem

**Check:**
- Clone stands straight
- Doesn't wobble
- Leaves don't touch soil

---

### Step 6: Create Microclimate

**Dome:**
1. Cover with dome/bottle
2. Leave small gap for ventilation (0.04-0.08 inch / 1-2 mm)

**Humidity:**
- Goal: 80-95%
- Spray inside dome 1-2 times daily

**Temperature:**
- Air: 72-79¬∞F (22-26¬∞C)
- Soil: 72-75¬∞F (22-24¬∞C, ideal with heating mat)

**Light:**
- CFL 20-40W or LED 50-100W
- 18-24 hours light
- Distance: 12-20 inches (30-50 cm)

---

### Step 7: Care (first 7-14 days)

**Daily:**

üìÖ **Ventilation:**
- Open dome for 5-10 minutes
- 1-2 times daily
- Prevents mold

üìÖ **Spraying:**
- Leaves: 1-2 times daily
- Inside dome: as needed
- Clean water pH 6.0

üìÖ **Check medium:**
- Should be moist (not wet!)
- If dry: spray surface
- Don't water heavily!

**Don't:**
‚ùå Don't pull out to check roots
‚ùå Don't move cups
‚ùå Don't water from top (only spray!)

---

### Step 8: Signs of Success

**Days 3-5:**
- Leaves firm (not wilted)
- Color green (not yellow)

**Days 7-10:**
- New growth (small leaves)
- This is a sign roots started growing!

**Days 10-14:**
- Roots visible through drainage holes
- Or through transparent cup walls

**Ready for transplant:**
‚úÖ Roots 0.8-2 inches (2-5 cm)
‚úÖ Clone actively growing
‚úÖ Firm leaves

---

### Step 9: Transition to Normal Conditions

**Acclimation (3-5 days):**

**Day 1-2:**
- Open dome for 1 hour, 2 times daily
- Gradually lower humidity

**Day 3-4:**
- Open dome for 2-3 hours
- Or make more holes

**Day 5:**
- Remove dome completely
- Clone ready for normal conditions

**Then:**
- Transplant to larger pot (1-3 L)
- Start light feeding (25% of normal)

## Conditions for Success

### Humidity

**Inside dome:**
- 80-95% (ideal 85-90%)

**How to measure:**
- Hygrometer (digital, $5-10)
- Or: condensation on dome walls = good

**Too much moisture:**
- Drops falling from dome onto leaves = mold
- Solution: ventilate more

---

### Temperature

**Air:**
- 72-79¬∞F (22-26¬∞C, optimal 75¬∞F / 24¬∞C)

**Soil (roots):**
- 72-75¬∞F (22-24¬∞C)
- Heating mat = +20% success

**Below 64¬∞F (18¬∞C):**
- Slow rooting (21+ days)

**Above 82¬∞F (28¬∞C):**
- Risk of bacteria, mold

---

### Watering

**First 3-5 days:**
- Only spray leaves and dome
- DON'T water soil!

**Days 5-14:**
- Spray soil surface (if dry)
- Don't flood!

**Check:**
- Finger in soil 0.4-0.8 inch (1-2 cm)
- Should be moist (not wet!)

---

### Lighting

**Intensity:**
- Low light! 20-40W
- Distance: 12-20 inches (30-50 cm)

**Schedule:**
- 18/6 or 24/0
- 24/0 better (roots grow faster without darkness)

**Don't use:**
‚ùå Powerful LED (100W+)
‚ùå HPS/MH lamps
‚ùå Direct sunlight

## Problems and Solutions

### Leaves Wilted (drooping)

**Causes:**
- Insufficient humidity
- Too bright light
- Soil dried out

**Solution:**
- Increase humidity (spray more often)
- Reduce light
- Check soil (should be moist)

---

### Leaves Yellowing

**If lower leaves:**
- Normal! Clone drops old leaves
- Focuses on roots

**If all leaves:**
- Too bright light
- Or: infection in medium
- Solution: reduce light, check sterility

---

### Mold on Soil Surface

**Cause:**
- Too high humidity
- Insufficient ventilation

**Solution:**
- Ventilate dome more often (3-4 times daily)
- Remove mold (carefully!)
- Spray surface with 3% hydrogen peroxide (1:10 with water)

---

### Stem Rots at Base

**Cause:**
- Soil overwatering
- Bacterial infection

**Solution:**
- Discard clone (can't save)
- Next time: water less
- Sterilize tools

---

### No Roots After 14+ Days

**Causes:**
- Cold soil (< 64¬∞F / 18¬∞C)
- Lack of hormones
- Bad cut

**Solution:**
- Use heating mat
- Next time: more hormone
- Check cutting technique
